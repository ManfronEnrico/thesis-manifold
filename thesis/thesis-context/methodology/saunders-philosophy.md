## 1. The framework

**Three assumption types** every philosophy makes:
- **Ontology** — nature of reality (objectivism/realism ↔ subjectivism/nominalism).
- **Epistemology** — what counts as acceptable knowledge (facts/numbers ↔ meanings/narratives).
- **Axiology** — role of values (value-free/detached ↔ value-bound/reflexive).

**Objectivism ↔ subjectivism** continuum; plus **regulation ↔ radical change** → Burrell & Morgan's
**4 paradigms**: functionalist (objectivist+regulation), interpretive (subjectivist+regulation), radical
structuralist, radical humanist.

**Five philosophies** (Table 4.3): positivism · critical realism · interpretivism · postmodernism ·
**pragmatism**.

**Approaches to theory development**: deduction (theory→data, positivism) · induction (data→theory,
interpretivism) · **abduction** (data↔theory, iterative; pragmatism/critical realism/postmodernism) ·
retroduction (critical realists' label for abduction).

## 2. Our position mapped

- **Philosophy = pragmatism (primary).** Saunders' pragmatism: "reality matters as the practical
  consequences of ideas; true theories/knowledge are those that enable successful action; research starts
  with a *problem* and aims at practical solutions that inform future practice; value-driven and reflexive;
  uses whatever methods (quant/qual/mixed) best answer the problem." This is a near-perfect fit for our
  **DSR** thesis ("knowledge is what works") and our problem-driven, Manifold-grounded framing — already
  the stance in Ch3 §3.1.
- **Approach = abduction** (build→test iteration of the artefact), with **deductive** elements in SRQ1
  (specialised-vs-pooled hypothesis testing). Saunders pairs abduction with pragmatism. Consistent with the
  onion note.
- **Paradigm placement**: **regulation** (we improve practice within existing structures, not radical
  change) and objectivist-leaning (quantitative, measurable) → closest to the **functionalist** quadrant;
  but pragmatism deliberately cuts across the objectivism–subjectivism dichotomy, so we note the
  functionalist leaning without being doctrinaire.
- **Axiology**: value-driven and reflexive (Saunders' pragmatism) — we make the choice of metrics,
  baseline, and evaluation design explicit rather than claiming value-freedom (already stated in Ch3 §3.1).

## 3. ⚠️ Key alignment point for Ch3 §3.1 (decision)

Ch3 §3.1 currently says **"pragmatism + moderate realism + empiricism."** Two issues vs Saunders:
1. **"Moderate realism" is not a Saunders label.** Worse, our ontology paragraph ("external business
   realities exist independently but are accessible only through measurement instruments that introduce
   assumptions/limitations") reads almost **verbatim like critical realism** (the *empirical* vs the *real*;
   stratified ontology). So we are nominally claiming pragmatism while writing a critical-realist ontology.
2. **Resolution (recommended):** lead unambiguously with **pragmatism** (the Saunders-named philosophy that
   fits DSR), and frame the realist ontology as *compatible with* pragmatism (pragmatism reconciles
   objectivism/subjectivism and is methodologically flexible) — drop the unlabelled "moderate realism".
   *Alternative:* explicitly adopt a **critical-realist ontology within a pragmatist orientation** and say
   so (Saunders notes critical realists use abduction/retroduction — consistent with our approach). Either
   is defensible; pick one and be consistent. Do **not** leave "moderate realism" unanchored.
