## 1. The research onion (6 layers, outer → inner)

| # | Layer | Saunders' options |
|---|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Philosophy** | positivism · critical realism · interpretivism · postmodernism · **pragmatism** |
| 2 | **Approach to theory development** | deduction · induction · **abduction** |
| 3 | **Methodological choice** | mono-method (qual/quant) · multi-method (qual/quant) · mixed methods (simple/complex) |
| 4 | **Strategy(ies)** | experiment · survey · ethnography · grounded theory · narrative inquiry · archival/documentary · **case study** · action research |
| 5 | **Time horizon** | cross-sectional (snapshot) · longitudinal (change over time) |
| 6 | **Procedures & techniques** | the actual data collection + analysis (the centre of the onion) |

**Key Saunders principles we rely on:**
- Research design = how you turn the research question/aim/objectives into a project; must be **coherent** across all layers and consistent with philosophy.
- **Research purpose** drives everything: exploratory · descriptive · **explanatory** · **evaluative** (or a combination).
  - Evaluative = "finds out how well something works"; signalled by **"To what extent…"** questions.
- Strategies are **not mutually exclusive** — you may combine them (e.g. experiment + case study).
- Pragmatism = choose methods for their usefulness in answering the problem; fits multi/mixed-method, problem-driven research.

## 3. The one structural tension: DSR is NOT a Saunders strategy

Saunders' eight strategies do **not** include Design Science Research. Resolution to state explicitly in Ch3:

> **DSR is the overarching methodology that *wraps* the research onion.** DSR (build + evaluate an
> artefact; Hevner 2004, Peffers 2007) is the frame; *within* it we operationalise Saunders' six onion
> layers. This is legitimate because (a) DSR sits naturally in Saunders' **pragmatist** paradigm, and
> (b) Saunders explicitly permits **combining strategies** — here Experiment + Case study are the
> vehicles through which the DSR evaluation is enacted.

Ready-to-use bridging sentences are drafted in chat (the "research onion" paragraph for §3.3).
