# Chapter 2: Literature Review

## Chapter Scope

This chapter provides the foundational literature review of the thesis. It establishes:
1. The landscape of predictive AI systems in business contexts
2. Existing decision-support approaches and their limitations
3. Key research gaps that motivate the RQ

## Research Questions Addressed

This chapter primarily addresses the **Main RQ**: How can AI systems be designed to provide reliable predictive decision-support in real-world business environments under computational constraints?

It sets context for all four SRQs:
- **SRQ1**: Which models and approaches exist? What are their efficiency trade-offs?
- **SRQ2**: How do existing systems coordinate multiple models?
- **SRQ3**: What role does context play in existing systems?
- **SRQ4**: How do current AI approaches compare to traditional BI?

## Paper Selection Criteria

Papers included in this chapter should cover:
- Predictive analytics and forecasting methods
- Business decision-support systems
- AI/ML applications in business
- Computational efficiency in ML systems
- Multi-agent or ensemble approaches
- Real-world deployment challenges

Papers should be marked for removal if they:
- Focus exclusively on theoretical ML without business context
- Address non-decision-support use cases (pure research)
- Are superseded by more recent comprehensive reviews
- Fall outside the domain of predictive systems
