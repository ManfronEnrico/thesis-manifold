## Core argument
Predictive ML models create quantifiable business value beyond descriptive analytics by shifting decision-making from reactive to proactive. Value is maximized when ML outputs are directly integrated into decision workflows (not standalone reports).

## Method
Meta-analysis of 45 business ML deployment case studies (retail, finance, healthcare, logistics) + practitioner survey (n=312) + five-stage ML integration maturity model synthesis.

## Key finding
Organizations integrating ML predictions directly into operational workflows achieve 23% higher decision accuracy and 41% faster response times vs. descriptive analytics baselines. Largest gains in high-frequency, time-sensitive decisions.

## Key quote
> "Organizations integrating ML predictions directly into operational decision workflows achieve 23% higher decision accuracy and 41% faster response times versus descriptive analytics, with the largest gains in high-frequency, time-sensitive decisions."

## Relevance to thesis
- SRQ4: foundational empirical benchmark for comparison with descriptive analytics; quantifies the business value differential
- Five-stage maturity model: positions thesis system as accelerating organizations from stage 2–3 (ad-hoc predictive) to stage 4–5 (integrated agentic decision support)

## Gap / limitation
Case studies predominantly from large enterprises with mature data infrastructure. Does not address computational constraints (SME environments), nor provide architectural guidance for ML-decision integration. → Thesis fills this gap.
