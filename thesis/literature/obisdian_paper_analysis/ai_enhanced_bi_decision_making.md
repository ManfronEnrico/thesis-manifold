## Core argument
Integrating AI predictive capabilities into traditional Business Intelligence systems enables a structural transition from retrospective reporting (descriptive BI) to forward-looking decision support (predictive BI), with measurable improvements in decision quality and user confidence.

## Method
Mixed-methods study: survey of 120 business analysts using AI-enhanced BI tools + case study implementation at 3 organisations. Measures: decision confidence, forecast adoption rate, self-reported decision quality improvement.

## Key finding
AI-enhanced BI users report 34% higher decision confidence and 28% higher forecast adoption rate vs. traditional descriptive BI. The key driver: AI provides an explanation alongside the prediction, not just a number.

## Relevance to thesis
- SRQ4 primary reference: directly describes the descriptive-to-predictive BI transition that the thesis implements

## Gap / limitation
Survey-based evidence — not an ML accuracy benchmark. AI tools evaluated are off-the-shelf BI platforms, not custom multi-agent systems. No RAM/compute constraints. No confidence calibration methodology.
