## Scope & Length

| Students | Max pages |
|---|---|
| 1 student | 80 standard pages |
| 2 students | 120 standard pages |
| 3 students | 160 standard pages |

- **Standard page** = max 2,275 characters incl. spaces on average
- A paper is too long if: exceeds page limit, violates margin/font rules, OR averages >2,275 chars/page

---

## Formatting

- **Margin**: at least 3 cm top and bottom; at least 2 cm left and right
- **Font size**: at least 11 points (footnotes can be smaller but must be readable)
- **Tables/diagrams/illustrations**: do NOT count toward character count, but DO count toward page space physically occupied
- **Headlines**: must be distinct and graphically different from body text
- **Page numbers**: required; Table of Contents must show where each main section begins

---

## What Counts Toward Page/Character Limits

### Counts in BOTH pages AND characters:
- Abstract
- Introduction, delimitation, research question
- Method section
- Footnotes and endnotes
- Conclusion and perspective

### Counts in pages ONLY (not characters):
- Table of contents

### Does NOT count at all:
- Front page
- Bibliography and list of sources
- Appendices

---

## Front Page Requirements

Must clearly state:
- [ ] Paper title (and subtitle if any)
- [ ] Type of paper
- [ ] Student name(s) — required if oral defence
- [ ] Student number
- [ ] Programme name
- [ ] Date of submission
- [ ] Supervisor name
- [ ] Group number (if relevant)
- [ ] Number of characters AND number of pages
- [ ] Whether the paper is confidential

## Abstract

- Must begin the thesis
- Max 1 page
- Counts toward total page count AND character count
- **Language (English programme)**: thesis and abstract in English

---

## Title Requirements

- English programme: English title only
- Danish programme: must have BOTH a Danish and an English title
- Must be registered in Digital Exam at submission

---

## References & Citations

- Must use a precise citation regime for all sources
- Reference style: confirm with supervisor (APA 7 recommended — CBS has Mendeley access)
- Plagiarism checked via Stopplagiat (stopplagiat.nu)
- CBS guide to academic integrity covers APA 7 standard

---

## Appendices

- NOT directly included in assessment; examiner may not read them
- Thesis must be self-contained — examiner must understand argumentation WITHOUT appendices
- Do NOT use appendices to hide important argumentation that did not fit
- Typical appendices: interviews, questionnaires, company data, strategy papers, reports
- Can be submitted separately or attached at end of thesis

---

## AI Use

- **Allowed** for master theses submitted after 1 February 2024
- **Autumn 2025 rules**: must declare use of AI when required by course/programme
- Check course description for specific rules

---

## Research Question (CBS guidance)

- Must be an academic question requiring theory and methods
- Should point to causes, not just observations
- RQ guides investigation AND communicates thesis scope to examiners
- Use the CBS Research Question Model: topic/wondering → theory, methodology, empirics, philosophy of science

---

## Research Design

- Must be a logical extension of the RQ
- Must match level of RQ (specific vs general)
- Options: Exploratory, Descriptive, Explanatory, Normative
- Structure of thesis should reflect the research design

---

## Case Study / Company Cooperation

- **Confidentiality agreement**: Must be signed BEFORE data access — not after
- If given access to confidential business data → company will likely require thesis to be **marked confidential** (= cannot be published)
- Deadline still applies even if cooperation delays — CBS does not grant extensions for this
- ⚠️ **Action required**: Clarify with Manifold AI before requesting Nielsen data:
  1. Is a confidentiality agreement required?
  2. Must the thesis be marked confidential?

---

## Philosophy of Science (CBS mandatory requirement)

- Thesis **must** reflect on both philosophy of science AND scientific methodology
- Must justify research design choices in relation to the RQ
- Recommended literature types: (1) general social science overview, (2) philosophy of science, (3) quantitative methods, (4) qualitative methods
- This is assessed by examiners — it is not optional

---

## GDPR

- Written consent required from all interview subjects before data collection
- Anonymisation removes GDPR applicability; pseudonymisation still falls under GDPR
