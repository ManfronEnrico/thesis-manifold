## Core argument
A systematic literature review of 97 DSR papers identifies four canonical artifact types (constructs, models, methods, instantiations) and proposes an extended taxonomy including design theories and process artifacts. Most DSR papers produce instantiations (working systems) but claim contributions at the model/method level — a classification mismatch that weakens theoretical contribution claims.

## Method
Systematic literature review of 97 DSR papers published in AIS basket journals and DESRIST proceedings (2004–2012). Coding scheme: Hevner et al. (2004) 4-type taxonomy + 3 additional types from literature. Inter-rater reliability: κ=0.82.

## Key finding
82% of DSR papers produce instantiation artifacts (working prototypes). Only 31% explicitly articulate the method/model-level contribution separable from the instantiation. Papers that articulate both levels receive significantly higher citation counts (mean 47 vs. 12 over 5 years). Extended taxonomy adds: design theories, process descriptions, and decision frameworks.

## Gap / limitation
Pre-LLM era — artifact taxonomy does not cover AI/LLM-specific artifact types (agents, prompts, calibration pipelines). Classification based on author self-report, not independent validation. Small corpus (97 papers) relative to current DSR literature volume.
