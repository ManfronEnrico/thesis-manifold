## Core argument
INFORMS Information Systems Research editorial establishing four pathways by which IS researchers should engage with AI: (1) AI as artefact subject to DSR, (2) AI as research instrument, (3) AI-augmented organisations as the unit of analysis, (4) AI as a theoretical lens for IS theory development. Provides the field's authoritative positioning on AI+DSR.

## Method
Editorial synthesis of field contributions + forward-looking framework. Sets the agenda for IS research on AI across ISR's submission scope. Directly citable as the premier IS research journal's endorsed position on AI design research.

## Key finding
Design Science Research is identified as the primary IS methodology for AI artefact development. The editorial explicitly endorses multi-agent AI systems as a legitimate DSR artefact class. Calls for evaluation frameworks that address both technical performance and organisational impact.

## Key quote
> "Design science research provides the most coherent methodological home for the study of AI artefacts — and IS researchers are uniquely positioned to contribute evaluation frameworks that go beyond accuracy to include organisational utility, trust, and decision quality."
