## Stationarity (ADF) and autocorrelation (ACF, per-brand-computed-then-averaged)

**Method note (corrected 2026-07-10)**: ACF is now computed per-brand (each
brand's own log-sales series only, no cross-brand concatenation), then
aggregated across brands via mean/median/IQR. This replaces an earlier pooled
brand-demeaned method that spliced all brands into one series before computing
ACF — that method had two concrete flaws: (1) boundary contamination (a
standard ACF run across concatenated brands spuriously correlates one brand's
last observation with the next brand's first), and (2) heteroscedasticity
(demeaning removes each brand's level but not its variance, so high-volume
brands dominated the pooled statistic). The new method is reproducible via
`thesis/data/preprocessing/nielsen_dvh/compute_acf_per_brand.py`.

**Coverage note**: this checkout's `_01_converted` data tier only has CSD and
energidrikke locally, and energidrikke's local snapshot is itself stale/partial
(29 periods on file vs. 39 claimed by `build_feature_matrix.py`'s
`regeneration_report.md`). CSD's local period count (44 raw) matches the
canonical script's figures, so CSD's numbers below are high-confidence.
energidrikke's are a directional check only (relaxed `MIN_PERIODS=20` instead
of the production `30`, since nothing clears 30 on this partial snapshot) —
treat as lower-confidence pending a full data refresh. danskvand and RTD have
no local raw/converted data in this checkout and are not yet computed by the
new method — re-run the script once their data is available; do not assume
their old pooled figures (row below, kept for reference) are correct.

## Reading

- Log transform is used for variance stabilisation; the **mean** is handled by
  differencing / lag features. Three of four category-level series are I(1)
  (non-stationary in log level, stationary after first differencing); RTD is
  already stationary in log level.
- Short-horizon autocorrelation is positive but **more moderate than
  previously reported** once corrected for the pooling artifact: CSD lag1
  mean +0.46 (median +0.55), energidrikke lag1 mean +0.37 (median +0.29).
  Still supports including lag_{1,2,3,4} features, but the previous framing
  ("strong" autocorrelation of +0.71 to +0.78) overstated the effect for at
  least these two categories.
- Lag-13 is consistently near zero or slightly negative under both the old and
  corrected methods (CSD −0.06, energidrikke −0.08) — this is now a
  **two-method-confirmed** finding, not just a pooled-method artifact, and
  raises a real open question (tracked separately) about whether the
  rolling-13/lag-13 "annual" feature in `build_feature_matrix.py` is earning
  its place given the data shows weak annual autocorrelation.
- Seasonality is sensible per category (CSD/RTD December, danskvand June for
  water, energidrikke March), supporting the calendar features
  (month/quarter/holiday_month).
- danskvand and RTD carry no promotional data (all-zero promo column) — an
  unmeasured-variable coverage limitation, to be framed as such in Ch4.
